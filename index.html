Introduction

Aruna Roy is a renowned Indian social activist and political leader. She is acclaimed for her work with the Mazdoor Kisan Shakti Sangathan (MKSS) and her pivotal role in the enactment of the Right to Information (RTI) Act in India. Her efforts have empowered millions by promoting transparency and accountability in governance.

Early Life

Aruna Roy was born on June 26, 1946, in Chennai, India. She pursued her education at Indraprastha College, University of Delhi, where she earned a degree in English literature. In 1968, she joined the Indian Administrative Service (IAS) but resigned after six years to work at the grassroots level, driven by her desire to effect meaningful social change.

Founding MKSS

In 1990, Aruna Roy co-founded the Mazdoor Kisan Shakti Sangathan (MKSS) in the village of Devdungri, Rajasthan. The organization focuses on empowering laborers and farmers, advocating for their rights, and ensuring transparency in government operations. MKSS's innovative use of social audits has been instrumental in exposing corruption and inefficiencies in government schemes.

Championing the RTI Act

Aruna Roy's advocacy was crucial in the passage of the Right to Information (RTI) Act in 2005. The RTI Act has revolutionized the way Indian citizens interact with their government, providing them the legal right to request information from public authorities. This legislation has been a powerful tool in combating corruption and fostering a culture of transparency.

Philanthropy and Social Justice

Beyond her work on RTI, Aruna Roy has been actively involved in numerous social justice initiatives. She has led campaigns for fair wages, social audits, and anti-corruption measures. Her work with MKSS has also included efforts to secure minimum wages for rural workers and to ensure effective implementation of government welfare programs.

Awards and Recognitions

Aruna Roy's contributions have been recognized globally. She has received numerous awards, including the Ramon Magsaysay Award for Community Leadership in 2000 and the Lal Bahadur Shastri National Award for Excellence in Public Administration, Academics and Management in 2010.
